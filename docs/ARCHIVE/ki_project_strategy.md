# Knowledge Item: Project Strategy, Deployment & Market Context

## Mission Statement

FarmSense is a "Deterministic Farming Operating System" — not a sensor company. Its primary legal and commercial mission is providing an **immutable Digital Water Ledger** defensible in Colorado Water Court.

## The Hydro-Economic Crisis (SLV Context)

- **Aquifer depletion**: 89,000 acre-feet/year, reservoir at 26% of historical capacity.
- **Pumping fee**: $500/acre-foot (quadrupled from $75–$150). 52 AF savings per pivot = **$26,000/pivot/year** directly justifying the $499/month Enterprise subscription.
- **Target**: 20–30% water reduction + 18–22% crop ROI increase.
- **Water Court deadline**: June 29, 2026 — Subdistrict 1 trial.

## 2-Field CSU Pilot Details

- **Location**: Center, Colorado — CSU San Luis Valley Research Center.
- **Hardware**: 2× PMT, 2× PFA, 2× VFA, 4–6× LRZ2 (Reference), 12–16× LRZ1 (Truth).
- **Purpose**: Generate empirical hydraulic ground truth for the June 2026 Subdistrict 1 Water Court trial.
- **Partners**: CSU SLV RC (research-grade lysimeters, weather stations, historical soil records).

## Subdistrict 1 Market Intelligence

- **~1,270–1,300** center-pivot fields (~126 acres average).
- **~160,000 acres** total irrigated.
- **Crop Mix**: 85% — Potatoes, Barley (Coors/Molson supply), Alfalfa.
- **TAM**: $7.6M ARR (Enterprise SaaS) + $3.66M one-time hardware.
- **Water fee savings unlocked**: $31.9M annually for farmers.
