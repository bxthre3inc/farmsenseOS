Overview\nThe Hydrologic Oracle is the inference engine that transforms raw sensor data into actionable irrigation intelligence.\n\n## 7.1 SPAC Thermodynamics: Surface Energy Balance\n\n### 7.1.1 Fundamental Equation\n```\nR_n - G = λE + H\n```\n\n| Term | Description | Measurement |\n|------|-------------|-------------|\n| R_n | Net radiation | Kipp & Zonen pyranometer (theoretical) |\n| G | Soil heat flux | VFA dual-needle thermal pulse (±1%) |\n| λE | Latent heat flux (ET) | Calculated from water balance |\n| H | Sensible heat flux | Calculated from temperature gradients |\n\n### 7.1.2 Penman-Monteith Model\n**Canopy Resistance (r_c):**\n```\nr_c = (Δ(R_n-G) + ρ_a c_p (e_s - e_a)/r_a) / (λE(Δ+γ)) - r_a\n```\n\n**Stomatal Closure Detection:**\n- VPD > 3.5 kPa → 40% photosynthetic efficiency reduction predicted\n- CWSI > 0.3 → Moderate stress, increase monitoring\n- CWSI > 0.6 → Severe stress, immediate irrigation\n\n## 7.2 Mathematical Derivation: Cokriging with Matern Kernels\n\n### 7.2.1 Why Matern?\n| Kernel | Smoothness | Use Case |\n|--------|------------|----------|\n| Exponential (ν=0.5) | Rough, continuous | Compaction ridges |\n| Matern (ν=1.0) | Once differentiable | Standard fields |\n| Gaussian (ν→∞) | Infinitely smooth | Not used (oversmooths) |\n\n**Auto-tuning:** ν varies 0.5-1.5 based on Field Roughness Index (FRI):\n- FRI < 0.3 → ν = 1.5 (smooth)\n- FRI 0.3-0.7 → ν = 1.0 (moderate)\n- FRI > 0.7 → ν = 0.5 (rough)\n\n### 7.2.2 Residual Calculation\n```\nZ(s) = m(s) + ε(s)\n```\n- m(s) = deterministic trend (satellite NDVI)\n- ε(s) = spatially correlated residual (ground-truth sensors)\n- Covariance: C(h) = σ² * (2^(1-ν) / Γ(ν)) * (κh)^ν * K_ν(κh)\n\n## 7.3 Crop-Specific Calibration Libraries\n\n### 7.3.1 Potato (Russet
