# ESTCP Grant Proposal: 1% Interpolation Physics

## 1. Project Title

**Project ID**: ER26-FS-01  
**Title**: Zero-Trust Spatial Water Management: Implementing 1% Interpolation Physics for Federal Resource Conservation.

## 2. Technical Objective

The primary objective is to demonstrate the **FarmSense RSS (Regional Superstation)** architecture's ability to achieve **<5% Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE)** in soil moisture interpolation across heterogeneous landscapes. By utilizing Ed25519-signed telemetry and FHE-encrypted Kriging tensors, we aim to provide an evidence-grade "Digital Water Ledger" for regulatory enforcement and conservation incentivization.

## 3. Technology Description

- **RSS Kriging Engine**: A Gaussian Process Regression engine optimized for 1m spatial grids, utilizing LOOCV (Leave-One-Out Cross-Validation) for real-time accuracy auditing.
- **AllianceChain PBFT**: A private, lightweight blockchain consensus mechanism ensuring non-repudiable audit trails for water rights transfers.
- **Dual-Layer Spatial Privacy**: Differential privacy and geometric jittering to enable anonymized research data sharing while maintaining high-fidelity localized management.

## 4. Expected Benefits

- **Water Savings**: Estimated 15-22% reduction in unmetered water consumption through VRI (Variable-Rate Irrigation) precision.
- **Legal Fidelity**: Reduction in Water Court litigation costs through deterministic, automated evidence generation.
- **Interoperability**: Standardized API for secondary water markets and carbon credit validation.

## 5. Implementation Roadmap

- **Phase I (March-June 2026)**: Pilot deployment at the Monte Vista CSU Extension site. Ground-truth calibration against VFA probes.
- **Phase II (July-Dec 2026)**: Basin-wide scaling across Subdistrict 1 (San Luis Valley). Integration with DWR remote sensing workflows.
- **Phase III (2027)**: Multi-state federal expansion (DOD/DOE resource management sites).
