40% photosynthetic reduction predicted |\n\n### 7.1.2 Soil Heat Flux (G) Measurement\n\n**VFA Dual-Needle Thermal Pulse Method:**\n- Upper needle: Heating element + temperature sensor\n- Lower needle: Temperature sensor only\n- Pulse: 8W for 30 seconds\n- Measurement: Temperature rise over time\n- Calculation: Thermal diffusivity → heat capacity → G\n\n**Accuracy:** ±1% of R_n (negligible error contribution)\n\n---\n\n## 7.2 Mathematical Derivation: Cokriging with Matérn Kernels\n\n### 7.2.1 Why Matérn?\n\nStandard variogram models (exponential, spherical) assume integer smoothness. SLV soil moisture exhibits:\n- Localized discontinuities (compaction ridges, wheel tracks)\n- Gradual transitions (texture boundaries)\n- Sharp changes (irrigation boundaries)\n\nThe Matérn family allows non-integer smoothness parameter ν:\n- ν = 0.5: Rough (exponential, suitable for wheel tracks)\n- ν = 1.5: Moderate (practical default)\n- ν = 2.5: Smooth (suitable for uniform fields)\n\n**Auto-Tuning:** Field Roughness Index (FRI) derived from:\n- Terrain slope variability\n- Historical yield CV\n- Traffic pattern density\n- Soil EC variability\n\n### 7.2.2 Residual Calculation\n\n**Universal Kriging Framework:**\n```\nZ(s) = m(s) + ε(s)\n```\n\nWhere:\n- **m(s)** = Deterministic trend (satellite covariates: NDVI, thermal, elevation)\n- **ε(s)** = Spatially correlated residual (ground-truth sensor interpolation)\n\n**Covariate Weighting:**\n| Source | Weight | Rationale |\n|--------|--------|-----------|\n| VFA (48") | 35% | Deep truth, stable |\n| LRZ2 (distributed) | 25% | Spatial coverage |\n| Sentinel-2 NDVI | 20% | Vegetation stress proxy |\n| Landsat thermal | 15% | ET estimation |\n| Elevation/slope | 5% | Topographic wetness |\n\n### 7.2.3 Cross-Validation Results\n\n| Metric | Target | Achieved |\n|--------|--------|----------|\n| R² | >0.90 | 0.94 |\n| MAPE | <5% | 3.2% |\n| RMSE | <0.05 VWC | 0.042 |\n| NRMSE | <10% | 7.8% |\n| Bias | ±0% | +0.3% |\n\n**Validation Method:** Leave-one-out cross-validation on 1,000 ground-truth points\n\n---\n\n## 7.3 Crop-Specific Calibration Libraries\n\n### 7.3.1 Potato (Russet Architecture\n\n### 7.4.1 LSTM Network Specification\n\n**Architecture:**\n- Input: 168 hours (7 days) of historical weather + 72 hours forecast\n- Layers: 2× LSTM (128 units each), 1× Dense (64 units), Output (1× ET₀)\n- Activation: tanh (LSTM), ReLU (Dense)\n- Optimizer: Adam (lr=0.001)\n- Loss: MSE\n\n**Training Data:**\n- SLV weather station records (10 years)\n- CSU AgWeatherNet data\n- 50,000 labeled samples (hourly)\n\n**Accuracy:**\n- 24-hour forecast: 94% R²\n- 72-hour forecast: 81% R²\n- 168-hour forecast: 72% R² (trend only)\n\n### 7.4.2 Ensemble Integration\n\n**Sources:**\n-
