grid cell:\n\n```\nR_n - G = λE + H\n```\n\nWhere:\n- **R_n** = Net radiation (solar + atmospheric, W/m²)\n- **G** = Soil heat flux (±1% accuracy via VFA dual-needle thermal pulse, W/m²)\n- **λE** = Latent heat flux (evapotranspiration, W/m²)\n- **H** = Sensible heat flux (air heating, W/m²)\n\n### 7.1.1 Penman-Monteith Model\n\nCalculates canopy resistance (r_c) to determine stomatal closure:\n\n```\nET₀ = [Δ(R_n - G) + ρₐ cₐ (eₛ - eₐ)/rₐ] / [Δ + γ(1 + rₛ/rₐ)]\n```\n\nWhere:\n- Δ = Slope of saturation vapor pressure curve (kPa/°C)\n- ρₐ = Air density (kg/m³)\n- cₐ = Specific heat of air (MJ/kg·°C)\n- eₛ - eₐ = Vapor pressure deficit (kPa)\n- rₐ = Aerodynamic resistance (s/m)\n- rₛ = Bulk surface resistance (s/m)\n- γ = Psychrometric constant (kPa/°C)\n\n**VPD Stress Thresholds:**\n| VPD (kPa) | Impact | Action |\n|-----------|--------|--------|\n| <1.0 | Optimal | None |\n| 1.0-2.5 | Mild stress | Monitor |\n| 2.5-3.5 | Moderate stress | Increase irrigation |\n| >3.5 | Severe stress | Emergency irrigation; 40% photosynthetic reduction predicted |\n\n### 7.1.2 Soil Heat Flux (G) Measurement\n\n**VFA Dual-Needle Thermal Pulse Method:**\n- Upper needle: Heating element + temperature sensor\n- Lower needle: Temperature sensor only\n- Pulse: 8W for 30 seconds\n- Measurement: Temperature rise over time\n- Calculation: Thermal diffusivity → heat capacity → G\n\n**Accuracy:** ±1% of R_n (negligible error contribution)\n\n---\n\n## 7.2 Mathematical Derivation: Cokriging with Matérn Kernels\n\n### 7.2.1 Why Matérn?\n\nStandard variogram models (exponential, spherical) assume integer smoothness. SLV soil moisture exhibits:\n- Localized discontinuities (compaction ridges, wheel tracks)\n- Gradual transitions (texture boundaries)\n- Sharp changes (irrigation boundaries)\n\nThe Matérn family allows non-integer smoothness parameter ν:\n- ν = 0.5: Rough (exponential, suitable for wheel tracks)\n- ν = 1.5: Moderate (practical default)\n- ν = 2.5: Smooth (suitable for uniform fields)\n\n**Auto-Tuning:** Field Roughness Index (FRI) derived from:\n- Terrain slope variability\n- Historical yield CV\n- Traffic pattern density\n- Soil EC variability\n\n### 7.2.2 Residual Calculation\n\n**Universal Kriging Framework:**\n```\nZ(s) = m(s) + ε(s)\n```\n\nWhere:\n- **m(s)** = Deterministic trend (satellite covariates: NDVI, thermal, elevation)\n- **ε(s)** = Spatially correlated residual (ground-truth sensor interpolation)\n\n**Covariate Weighting:**\n| Source | Weight | Rationale |\n|--------|--------|-----------|\n| VFA (48") | 35% | Deep truth, stable |\n| LRZ2 (distributed) | 25% | Spatial coverage |\n| Sentinel-2 NDVI | 20% | Vegetation stress proxy |\n| Landsat thermal | 15% | ET estimation |\n| Elevation/slope | 5% | Topographic wetness |\n\n### 7.2.3 Cross-Validation Results\n\n| Metric | Target | Achieved |\n|--------|--------|----------|\n| R² | >0.90 | 0.94 |\n| MAPE | <5% | 3.2% |\n| RMSE | <0.05 VWC | 0.042 |\n| NRMSE | <10% | 7.8% |\n| Bias | ±0% | +0.3% |\n\n**Validation Method:** Leave-one-out cross-validation on 1,000 ground-truth points\n\n---\n\n## 7.3 Crop-Specific Calibration Libraries\n\n### 7.3.1 Potato (Russet
